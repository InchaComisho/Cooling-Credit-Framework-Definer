## What Is a Cooling Credit?

A Cooling Credit is a new value-evaluation concept that assesses not only CO₂ emission reductions, but also the actual reduction of heat load and the restoration of natural cooling functions in local and planetary systems.

Conventional carbon credits primarily answer the following questions:

```text
How much CO₂ was reduced?
How much CO₂ was absorbed?
How much CO₂ was offset?
```

Cooling Credits answer different questions:

```text
How much was actually cooled?
How much was surface temperature reduced?
How much was urban heat load reduced?
How much water circulation was restored?
How much soil water-retention capacity was recovered?
How much plant evapotranspiration was restored?
How much ocean circulation was improved?
How much natural cooling feedback was restarted?
```

Therefore, Cooling Credit is not merely carbon accounting.

It is **thermal accounting**, **valuation of natural cooling functions**, and **a measurement protocol for planetary circulation regeneration**.

## Difference Between Carbon Credits and Cooling Credits

Carbon Credits mainly evaluate CO₂ reduction, absorption, and offsetting.  
Cooling Credits evaluate measured cooling, heat-load reduction, and recovery of natural cooling functions.

The central unit of Carbon Credits is carbon quantity.  
The central units of Cooling Credits include temperature, heat load, water circulation, water retention, evapotranspiration, ocean circulation, and local co-benefits.

The primary purpose of Carbon Credits is to balance or offset emissions.  
The primary purpose of Cooling Credits is to actually cool the Earth and local regions.

Carbon Credits typically evaluate forest absorption, renewable energy, and emissions reduction.  
Cooling Credits evaluate urban cooling, soil regeneration, forest regeneration, ocean cooling, desert-edge regeneration, and related restoration systems.

In essence, Carbon Credit is carbon accounting.  
Cooling Credit is thermal accounting and natural circulation accounting.

Carbon Credits are not unnecessary.

But they are not enough.

Even if CO₂ is reduced, Earth's cooling capacity will not be sufficiently restored if cities continue storing heat, soils remain dry, forests weaken, ocean surfaces warm, and water cycles remain broken.

This is why Cooling Credits are needed.

## MRV: Measurement, Reporting, Verification

MRV means Measurement, Reporting, and Verification.

For Cooling Credits, important data may include air temperature, surface temperature, humidity, WBGT, soil moisture, soil organic matter, plant evapotranspiration, vegetation coverage, rainwater storage volume, reclaimed water use, sea-surface temperature, dissolved oxygen, chlorophyll, plankton indicators, forest cover, urban green coverage, energy load, and medical, evacuation, and disaster-risk indicators.

Cooling Credits should not be issued based on mood, image, or symbolic claims.
