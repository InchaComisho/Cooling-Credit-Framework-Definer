# Aerosol Shading Is Not Cooling

## Exclusion Principle for Shading-Based Geoengineering in the Cooling Credit Framework

## Overview

This document defines the logic for excluding stratospheric aerosol injection, sulfur aerosol dispersal, solar radiation management, and simple shading-based geoengineering from eligibility under the Cooling Credit Framework.

The central thesis is:

> Aerosol shading is not cooling.  
> Cooling means restoring water circulation, soil moisture, evapotranspiration, cloud formation, rainfall, wet deposition, forests, wetlands, rivers, oceans, microorganisms, ecosystems, and Earth's natural heat-release, atmospheric-cleaning, and cooling feedback systems.

Therefore, any intervention that merely reduces sunlight while failing to restore water circulation, soil moisture, evapotranspiration, rainfall-based atmospheric cleaning, and natural cooling feedbacks shall not qualify as a Cooling Credit.

---

## Background: Do Not Look Only at Sulfur Aerosols

Many proposals for stratospheric aerosol injection are centered on sulfur or sulfate aerosols because they attempt to mimic the temporary cooling observed after large volcanic eruptions.

However, the real atmosphere is not composed of sulfur alone.

The atmosphere already contains desert dust, Asian dust, road dust, pollen, spores, smoke, soot, sea salt, mineral particles, combustion-derived particles, biogenic particles, PM2.5, and complex particles that may not be fully classified or understood.

Therefore, it is dangerous to judge Earth's radiation balance, clouds, rainfall, heat release, and atmospheric cleaning only through the increase or decrease of sulfur aerosols.

The essential questions are:

```text
Which particles already exist in the atmosphere?
At what altitudes are they distributed?
Which particles scatter light, and which absorb heat?
Which particles alter clouds and rainfall?
Which particles are removed by rain?
Which particles are resuspended from dry surfaces?
How much do wetlands, forests, rivers, oceans, and moist soils trap these particles?
```

Adding artificial particles without answering these questions is not cooling.

It is an additional intervention into an already complex atmospheric particle system.

## Atmospheric Particle Saturation and Resuspension Loop

The core concept proposed here is the **Atmospheric Particle Saturation and Resuspension Loop**.

It refers to the following causal structure:

```text
Global warming, land degradation, and forest loss
↓
Soil drying, bare land expansion, and wetland loss
↓
Dust, fine particles, pollen, smoke, and PM-like particles become easier to lift into the air
↓
When rainfall becomes localized or reduced, wet deposition weakens
↓
Particles are not sufficiently washed out by rain
↓
Deposited particles are resuspended from dry surfaces
↓
Atmospheric particle loading remains chronically elevated
↓
Clouds, rainfall, radiation, health, agriculture, and transportation are affected
↓
Water circulation and natural cooling feedbacks weaken further
```

This does not mean that every particle remains in the air forever.

Many tropospheric particles can be removed within days to weeks through deposition and precipitation. Particles that enter the stratosphere may remain longer because they are less easily washed out by rain.

However, if sources increase, rainfall cleaning weakens, surface fixation declines, and resuspension continues from dry land, atmospheric particle loading can remain chronically high.

Adding artificial sulfur aerosols under such conditions does not repair the broken circulation.

## Rain Is Earth's Atmospheric Cleaning System

Rain is Earth's atmospheric cleaning system.

Raindrops capture dust, fine particles, pollen, smoke, and PM-like particles and carry them to the land surface, rivers, wetlands, and oceans.

This process can be described as wet deposition or atmospheric cleaning by rain.

However, when rainfall becomes localized, long dry periods increase, surfaces dry out, and wetlands or forests decline, particles are less effectively washed out.

Particles deposited on dry surfaces can then be lifted again by wind, vehicles, turbulence, and dry surface heating.

The issue is not merely that particles exist in the air.

The issue is that Earth's atmospheric cleaning system is weakening, and the circulation that returns particles to soils, water systems, and ecosystems is breaking down.

---

## Moist Soils, Wetlands, Forests, and Oceans Are Natural Particle Traps

Earth originally has multiple natural particle traps:

```text
moist soils
humus
wetlands
rivers
lakes
oceans
forest leaf surfaces
grasslands
microbially active soil structures
plant root systems
```

These systems fix deposited particles, suppress resuspension, and return matter to soils, water systems, and ecosystems.

When land dries, forests decline, wetlands disappear, and soil organic matter decreases, particles are no longer fixed.

They remain as dry powder on the surface.

When wind blows, they return to the atmosphere.

Under such conditions, adding particles to the upper atmosphere is not a root solution.

The root solution is to regenerate moist surface traps.

---

## Shading Is Not Cooling

Shading is an operation that reduces part of incoming sunlight.

Cooling is the release of accumulated heat and the restoration of water circulation and natural cooling functions.

These are not the same.

Shading-based geoengineering does not solve the following root problems:

```text
increased CO₂ concentration
ocean acidification
dry soils
lost humus
weakened microbial circulation
reduced evapotranspiration
broken water cycles
weakened rainfall
dust and fine-particle source regions
decline of particle-trapping functions in wetlands, rivers, forests, and oceans
urban heat storage
ocean surface heat accumulation
```

Therefore, shading may temporarily mask symptoms, but it does not repair Earth's cooling system.

Cooling Credits should evaluate measured cooling effects and the recovery of natural cooling feedbacks, not shading itself.

---

## Cooling Credit Exclusion Principle

This framework adopts the following principle:

> Any intervention that merely reduces sunlight while failing to restore water circulation, soil moisture, evapotranspiration, rain-based atmospheric cleaning, wet deposition, surface fixation, natural particle traps, and natural cooling feedbacks shall not qualify as a Cooling Credit.

This principle distinguishes Cooling Credits from simple shading, albedo manipulation, radiation management, and geoengineering interventions that do not restore planetary circulation.

---

## What Cooling Credits Should Evaluate Instead

True cooling is not adding particles to block sunlight.

True cooling is restoring the following functions:

```text
rain washing the atmosphere
moist soils fixing particles
forests reducing wind and dust
wetlands absorbing particles and nutrients
rivers carrying particles toward the ocean
oceans circulating heat and particles
plants moving heat through evapotranspiration
clouds and rainfall cooling the surface
microorganisms and humus holding water
```

Activities that restore these functions can qualify as Cooling Credit candidates.

---

## Conclusion

Stratospheric aerosol injection and sulfur aerosol dispersal do not repair Earth's cooling system.

They do not restore water circulation, soil moisture, evapotranspiration, cloud formation, rainfall, wet deposition, particle fixation by moist surfaces, or natural particle traps such as forests, wetlands, rivers, and oceans.

If Earth is already drying and dust, fine particles, pollen, smoke, and PM-like particles are increasingly easy to lift into the air, then adding artificial particles is a partial optimization that ignores the whole circulation system.

Cooling Credit does not recognize shading as cooling.

Cooling Credit evaluates actions that actually cool, release heat, clean the atmosphere, restore water circulation, and restart natural cooling feedbacks.
